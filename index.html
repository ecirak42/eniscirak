About me

My name is Enis Cirak.

I had panic attacks, depersonalization, and bad anxiety for about four or five years.

It changed how I lived. I started avoiding social situations and meetings. I got paranoid about trying new foods. I was overstimulated most of the time and didn’t feel like myself. The calm, confident version of me felt far away.

I know what it’s like to plan your day around whether your body is going to cooperate. I know what it’s like to sit in a room full of people and feel like you’re the only one who can’t just be there.

What got me out wasn’t numbing it, fighting it, or outrunning it. It was turning toward it. When you actually turn toward what you’re feeling, instead of bracing against it, it starts to move on its own.

That’s the work I do with people now. I remember how painful it is to watch your world get smaller, and I want to help other people move through it with more clarity and steadiness.

We go at your pace. No rushing, no pushing. Just honest conversation and real work.

Influences: Internal Family Systems, Michael Singer, Eckhart Tolle, Adyashanti, David Burns.

FAQ

Is this therapy?

No. This is coaching. It is not therapy, and it is not a medical service.

Do I need to have agoraphobia for this to help?

No. This work can also help if you have panic attacks, fear of fear, ongoing anxiety, or avoidance that is starting to shape your life.

Do you use exposure?

Not in a pushy or rigid way. Exposure helps a lot of people. If it is part of what you are doing, this work can go alongside it. What I focus on is understanding the pattern, and changing how you relate to what is happening underneath.

What happens in a session?

We talk about what is happening, but we do not just stop at talking. We slow down to notice what is going on in your body, your mind, and your emotions, as it happens. Then we work with what is actually driving the fear.

Can I still work with you if I am in therapy or on medication?

Yes. Many people are. This work can sit alongside therapy or medication. Medication decisions stay between you and your doctor.

How long does it take?

It depends on the person and the pattern. Some people feel clearer quickly. For others it takes longer. The point is to do real work that actually helps, not to force a timeline.
