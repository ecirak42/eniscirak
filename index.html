01 · Where you might be

You are not broken. Your world has just gotten smaller.

Agoraphobia can make life feel like a shrinking room. You may look fine on the outside while, inside, you’re scanning for exits, bracing for symptoms, and trying to stay one step ahead of the next panic attack.

That’s exhausting. It can start to feel like you’re not really living — just managing, avoiding, enduring.

And the part of you that got this vigilant isn’t broken either. It’s been trying, in its own way, to protect you. We’re not going to fight it. We’re going to listen to it.

This isn’t the end of your life. It’s a pattern — and patterns, even old protective ones, can change.

02 · The real problem

It’s a feedback loop — and loops can be interrupted.

One little sensation becomes a thought. The thought creates fear. The fear releases adrenaline. And suddenly you’re caught inside something that feels bigger than you.

  1. 01 A sensation. A skipped heartbeat, a wave of dizziness, a tight chest.
  2. 02 A thought. “What if this becomes a panic attack?”
  3. 03 Fear. Which releases adrenaline into the body.
  4. 04 Louder sensations. And the loop tightens.

Seeing the loop is the first step. But naming it isn’t the same as healing it. Real change happens when you also turn toward what’s underneath it — the emotion, the part of you that’s been bracing — and let it finally move through.

03 · What we’ll work on

Five things you’ll learn, together.

  1. How to recognise panic as a pattern passing through you — not as who you are.
  2. How to meet the anxious part of you with curiosity, instead of going to war with it.
  3. How to release the emotion and energy that’s been stuck underneath the fear.
  4. How to stay present, even while your body is loud.

04 · About

I’ve been on the other side of this.

I’ve lived through anxiety in my own life. I know how overwhelming and limiting it feels when fear starts shaping every decision — where you go, who you see, whether you leave the house.

My work draws on Internal Family Systems, Michael Singer’s teaching on releasing stored emotion, the present‑moment clarity of Eckhart Tolle and Adyashanti, and the practical cognitive tools of Dr. David Burns. The thread through all of it: we don’t numb, fight, or outrun what you’re feeling. We turn toward it — gently — and let it resolve at the root.

Part of why I do this is because I remember how painful it is to watch your world get smaller. I want to help other people move through that same struggle with more clarity, steadiness, and hope.

We’ll go at your pace. No rushing, no pushing, no forcing. Just honest conversation and real, root‑level work.

05 · Common questions

A few things people often ask.

What if I can’t leave the house for the call?

You don’t need to. Every call happens online, from wherever feels safest.

How is this different from CBT or standard anxiety therapy?

We use some cognitive tools — especially for understanding the feedback loop. But the work also goes deeper: into the parts of you that are generating the fear, the emotion stored underneath, and the presence you can build to meet it all without being swept away. The goal isn’t to manage symptoms. It’s to resolve what’s driving them.

Do I have to stop my medication?

No, and I’d never advise that. This work sits alongside whatever you’re already doing. Medication decisions stay between you and your doctor.

What does a session actually look like?

A calm conversation. We map what’s happening in your specific case, work gently with the parts of you that are reacting, and make room for what’s been stuck to move. No exposure drills, no pushing, no forcing.

Is the first call really free?

Yes — a free 30‑minute conversation. No obligation. If it doesn’t feel like the right fit, you leave with a clearer picture of what’s going on, and nothing else.

How long does this take?

It varies. Most people feel a meaningful shift within the first few weeks, as their relationship with panic starts to change. Full integration — feeling steady, not just managing — takes longer. We go at your pace.
