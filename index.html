Who this is for

People whose world has gotten smaller because of panic or agoraphobia, who have tried managing the symptoms, and who are ready to work on what is underneath.

What you are dealing with

A loop.

  1. 01 A sensation in the body. A skipped beat. A wave of lightheadedness. A tight chest.
  2. 02 A thought. What if this is the one.
  3. 03 Fear. Which the body reads as real danger and answers with adrenaline.
  4. 04 A louder sensation. Which confirms the fear. Which releases more adrenaline.

The loop is self-sustaining. That is why willpower does not break it. Why avoidance makes it worse. Why “just calm down” is useless.

Seeing the loop is the first thing we do. It helps. But it is not enough on its own. The loop runs on something. Usually an older feeling that has been waiting, often for years, to be met. The real work is there.

What we do about it

We meet online, one to one. We find the place in your body where the fear lives. We stay there together, without force, until what has been stuck begins to move.

No exposure drills. No homework. No protocol you have to follow between sessions.

Most people feel a real shift in their relationship to panic within the first few weeks. Feeling fully at ease in yourself takes longer than that. You set the pace.

Why this works when other things have not

Most approaches stop at the mind. They teach you the loop and send you home with breathing exercises. The loop keeps running because the feeling underneath it was never touched.

This work goes underneath. It is slower in the first week and faster in the tenth. It does not ask you to push through anything. It asks you to turn toward what is already there. That is what the nervous system has been waiting for.

About

I’ve lived through anxiety in my own life. I know how overwhelming and limiting it feels when fear starts shaping every decision. Where you go. Who you see. Whether you leave the house.

My work is informed by Internal Family Systems, Michael Singer, Eckhart Tolle, Adyashanti, and Dr. David Burns. They all point to the same thing. You do not need to numb what you are feeling, fight it, or outrun it. You can turn toward it. When you do, it begins to move on its own.

Part of why I do this is because I remember how painful it is to watch your world get smaller. I want to help other people move through that same struggle with more clarity, steadiness, and hope.

We go at your pace. No rushing. No pushing. Just honest conversation, and real work.

Do I need to stop my medication?

No. That is between you and your doctor.

What does a session actually look like?

A quiet conversation, mostly. We look at what is happening in your specific case, and we work with what the moment asks for.

Some days this means slowing down and finding a sensation in your body, and staying with it until it shifts. Other days it is about meeting a part of you that has been carrying something alone for a long time. Other times we look at a thought loop and turn toward what is underneath it.

The practices vary. The aim is steady. To release what has been stuck, and come back to yourself.

How long until I feel different?

Most people notice a real change within the first few weeks. Feeling fully steady takes longer. We go at your pace.
